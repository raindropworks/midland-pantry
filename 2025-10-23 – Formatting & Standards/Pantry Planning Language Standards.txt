PANTRY PLANNING PROJECT – LANGUAGE & TRANSLATION STANDARDS
Version 1.0 · October 24 2025
Midland Pantry & Community Food Forest Initiative

PURPOSE
--------
This document defines language accessibility and translation guidelines
for all Pantry Planning materials to ensure inclusive communication with
the Multnomah County community.

LANGUAGE PRIORITIES
-------------------
Primary working language: English  
Secondary and translation priority languages (based on Oregon.gov listings):
• Spanish (Español)  
• Vietnamese (Tiếng Việt)  
• Chinese – Simplified (中文 简体)  
• Russian (Русский)  
• Ukrainian (Українська)  
• Somali (Af-Soomaali)  

Each document will eventually include translated editions or summaries
in these languages, depending on community need and funding.

TRANSLATION FORMAT
------------------
• Keep the same page layout, section order, and footnote structure.  
• Use professional or community translators familiar with cultural context.  
• Include translator attribution on the cover page.  
• Maintain the revision table and version numbering consistent with
  the English edition.

ACCESSIBILITY IN LANGUAGE
--------------------------
• Use plain, clear language whenever possible.  
• Avoid idioms, legal jargon, or complex sentence structures.  
• Provide visual aids or diagrams alongside translated text.  
• Test digital copies with screen readers in both English and translated versions.  

DIGITAL FORMAT
---------------
All translations will be maintained in UTF-8 encoding for compatibility
with Linux, macOS, and Windows systems.
